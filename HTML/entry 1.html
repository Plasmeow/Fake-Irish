A brief version of the story

Fionn MacCumhail, known for his many exploits in The Boyhood Deeds of Fionn, was a central figure during the Irish Fenian Cycle. One of the most famous stories from The Boyhood Deeds of Fionn is The Salmon of Knowledge, which we will be going through today.


Irish mythology depicts a hazel tree as the first thing to come into existence, with its branches containing the knowledge of the entire universe. This very hazel tree stretched over the Well of Wisdom, and in this well lived a fish, an ordinary salmon. Whenever a hazelnut would fall into the well, the fish would eat them and, in turn, gain the knowledge of the entire universe. The prophecy goes that the first to catch and eat said salmon would gain its knowledge.

After 7 years of trying and failing, a man by the name of Finegas managed to catch it. Finegas instructed his apprentice, Fionn, to prepare the fish for him but to never have a taste of it. In the process of preparing it, he touched the fish to check if it was done, and got burning fish fat all over his thumb. This led to the boy putting his thumb in his mouth to soothe the pain. Once he brought the fish back to Finegas, he noticed a new-found shine of wisdom in the boy's eyes, proceeding to ask Fionn if he'd had a taste of its flesh. Fionn responded no, and explained what happened. Finegan, realizing he had gained the salmon's knowledge, let him have the fish.


From then on, Fionn would draw upon this knowledge by biting his thumb.


My personal connection to it

I remember when grandmother told me about this story at the ripe age of 7. We would visit quite often back then. That dingy little appartment in the outskirts of the city was where I created most of my early memories. Can't say my 7-year-old self remembers anything but "this guy became wise when he bit his thumb", evidenced by the fact that I started biting my thumb when I was trying really hard to concentrate on doing my homework. So much so that I have an extra visible line on it from doing it too often. It's a bit embarassing, but I still find myself biting my thumb in my very adult years.


Note: there are different versions of this story, but they all end the same way.
